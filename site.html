05عن الاستوديو

عن الاستوديو

نسج فريقٌ يدخل الغرفة بعد أن ينفضّ الاجتماع، ويسأل كلّ شخصٍ فيها سؤالاً واحدًا: ماذا فهمتَ أنت تحديدًا؟ ثم يبني من الإجابات نسخةً واحدة يقدر الجميع أن يتصرّف بها.

لا نجمع معلوماتٍ أكثر. نكتب الإجابات المتناقضة جنبًا إلى جنب حتى تصير قابلةً للفعل.
دورنا

لا نملك المعرفة، ولا ننوب عن أصحابها. نقرّر أي إجابةٍ تدخل الصورة النهائية وأيّها تُترك — وهذا قرارٌ نتحمّل مسؤوليته صراحةً.

مبادئ التشغيل
  1. 01نبدأ بسؤال كلّ شخصٍ في الغرفة قبل أن نقترح أي حل.
  2. 02نعرض كيف وصلنا للنتيجة، لا النتيجة وحدها؛ نقول أين اختلفت الإجابات ولماذا.
  3. 03ما يصمد بعد أن يعترض عليه ثلاثةٌ من خمسة، هو وحده ما يستحقّ الثقة.
  4. 04كل أداةٍ ننتجها قابلة للإلغاء؛ إن توقّفت عن مساعدة فريقٍ على التصرّف، نتخلّى عنها.
  5. 05لا نملك الكلمة الأخيرة؛ ما يحدث فعلاً في الغرفة بعدنا هو الذي يقرّر.
  6. 06حين يختلف اثنان في نفس الغرفة، لا نصوّت لإنهاء الخلاف؛ نكتب كلا الموقفين ونستمر.
  7. 07القدرة على التصرّف الجماعي تُبنى في تلك الغرفة، لا تُكتشف جاهزةً فيها.
كيف نعمل

ثلاثة أنماط تدخّل، تُستدعى حسب ما يحدث فعلاً في الغرفة، لا حسب قائمة خدمات ثابتة.

02work

Work

An orientation, not a portfolio. Each engagement is evidence of understanding that held its weight when it was time to act — cases, frameworks and living tools maintained with the field.

what nasj weaves

Four forms recur under every engagement — not services picked from a menu, but one structure taking different shapes to fit the situation.

Field synthesis

We trace what is scattered across complex systems — reports, lived experience, evidence — and weave it into a form others can use. Produces: knowledge briefs · source maps

Frameworks & models

We turn an ambiguous field into a structure that organises understanding and holds under the pressure of reality — not a taxonomy that reassures without explaining. Produces: frameworks · conceptual models

Diagnostic tools

We put what we learn into tools others can pick up and use to read their own situation, without the author standing over their shoulder. Produces: situation-reading tools · diagnostic kits

Studios & sessions

We design how collective understanding forms, not how content gets delivered — and hold the room so it survives a real group whose behaviour can't be scripted. Produces: participation structures · learning experiences

05about

About the studio

Nasj is an intellectual environment working between understanding and action. It does not add more information; it weaves the scattered into structure that can bear a decision and collective action.

Nasj does not add more information; it builds what holds it together — a structure that can bear action when the time comes.
the weaver's role

The weaver holds neither the knowledge nor its owners' authority; they decide what enters and what is left out — choosing is as much the work as connecting.

operating principles
  1. 01From relations before outputs, and from system before intervention.
  2. 02We show the thinking, not only the results; we say where the weave tore, and why.
  3. 03Only what survives after reality has torn it and it has been rewoven earns trust.
  4. 04A structure is a tool, not a truth; once it stops adding understanding or improving action, it becomes part of the problem it was woven to solve.
  5. 05A structure does not have the final word; only reality does.
  6. 06Organised disagreement is a thread in the weave, not a cut in it.
  7. 07Collective agency is woven, not discovered.
how we work

Three patterns of intervention, not three departments — each is called on when its condition appears in the field.

A narrative pattern

When narratives fracture around one reality, and each side keeps its own separate account.

A participation pattern

When understanding needs to be woven collectively, not handed down ready-made from one person to others.

A knowledge pattern

When knowledge exists, but can't be carried or used over time.

who is behind nasj?

Nasj began from a pattern noticed across different rooms: teams, coalitions, initiatives, all arriving at the same moment — a visible agreement hiding several different pictures of what was said and settled.

Those rooms weren't short of intelligence or intent, but of something simpler: someone returning to the same meeting, asking everyone in it the same question, then placing the answers side by side.

behind nasj:
Abdalmohimen Ismail

A researcher and learning designer working at the intersection of political economy, social organizing, and knowledge design.

This is where Nasj was born — not a side project, but a studio dedicated to one question:
how can a group of people see the same reality, before trying to change it?

This work needs a discipline no one person can hold alone between other tasks.
